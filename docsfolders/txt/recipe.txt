# Vibrant Harvest Medley with Seasonal Fruits & Vegetables

## Ingredients

### Vegetables
- 2 medium zucchini, diced into ½-inch cubes (about 2 cups)
- 1 large eggplant, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes (about 3 cups)
- 2 bell peppers (1 red, 1 yellow), seeded and cut into 1-inch pieces
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 3 medium carrots, peeled and sliced into ¼-inch rounds (about 1½ cups)
- 1 large red onion, coarsely chopped
- 2 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
- 2 cups broccoli florets, cut into bite-sized pieces

### Fruits
- 2 medium apples (Honeycrisp or Gala), cored and diced into ½-inch pieces
- 1 cup blueberries, rinsed
- 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced into ½-inch pieces
- 1 cup pineapple chunks, fresh or canned (if canned, drain well)
- 2 oranges, peeled and segmented
- 3 kiwis, peeled and sliced

### Rice
- 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed thoroughly
- 3½ cups vegetable broth or water

### Seasonings & Other Ingredients
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup
- 1 tablespoon ground cumin
- 2 teaspoons ground coriander
- 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
- ½ teaspoon cinnamon
- ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
- 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
- 2 tablespoons fresh orange juice
- ¼ cup fresh herbs (combination of mint, cilantro, and parsley), chopped
- 2 tablespoons toasted sesame seeds
- 1 teaspoon sea salt (plus more to taste)
- ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar

## Preparation Instructions

### Preparing the Rice
1. In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed basmati rice and vegetable broth.
2. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
3. Once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for 15-18 minutes until liquid is absorbed.
4. Remove from heat and let stand, covered, for 5 minutes.
5. Fluff with a fork and set aside.

### Vegetable Preparation
1. Wash and prepare all vegetables as described in the ingredients list.
2. In a small bowl, mix 1 teaspoon cumin, 1 teaspoon coriander, ½ teaspoon turmeric, ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper, ½ teaspoon salt, and ¼ teaspoon black pepper.
3. In a large bowl, toss the zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, carrots, onion, and broccoli with 2 tablespoons olive oil and the spice mixture until well coated.

### Fruit Preparation
1. Wash and prepare all fruits as described in the ingredients list.
2. In a medium bowl, gently toss the prepared fruits with 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup, 1 tablespoon lemon juice, and 1 tablespoon orange juice.
3. Set aside to marinate while preparing the rest of the dish.

## Cooking Methods

### Oven Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper or aluminum foil.
3. Spread the seasoned vegetables in a single layer on the baking sheet.
4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
5. During the last 5 minutes of roasting, add the cherry tomatoes and spinach to the baking sheet.
6. Remove from oven and set aside.

### Crock Pot Instructions
1. Place the seasoned vegetables (except spinach and cherry tomatoes) in a 6-quart slow cooker.
2. Add ½ cup vegetable broth, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, minced garlic, and grated ginger.
3. Cook on LOW for 4-5 hours or on HIGH for 2-3 hours, until vegetables are tender.
4. During the last 30 minutes of cooking, stir in the spinach and cherry tomatoes.
5. If the mixture seems too watery, leave the lid off for the last 30 minutes to allow some liquid to evaporate.

### Microwave Instructions
*Note: For microwave cooking, you'll need to cook vegetables in smaller batches.*

1. Place harder vegetables (carrots, broccoli) in a microwave-safe bowl with 2 tablespoons of water. Cover with a microwave-safe lid or plate.
2. Microwave on HIGH for 3-4 minutes.
3. In a separate microwave-safe bowl, place zucchini, eggplant, and bell peppers with 2 tablespoons of water. Cover.
4. Microwave on HIGH for 2-3 minutes.
5. In a third bowl, place spinach and cherry tomatoes. Cover.
6. Microwave on HIGH for 1 minute.
7. Drain any excess water from all vegetable batches.
8. Mix all microwaved vegetables with the spice mixture and 1 tablespoon olive oil.

## Final Assembly

### Creating the Sauce
1. In a small saucepan, heat 1 tablespoon olive oil over medium heat.
2. Add remaining minced garlic and grated ginger, sautéing for 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add remaining cumin, coriander, turmeric, and cinnamon. Stir for 30 seconds.
4. Pour in 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup, remaining lemon juice, orange juice, soy sauce, and rice vinegar.
5. Simmer for 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened.
6. Remove from heat and set aside.

### Combining All Elements
1. In a large serving bowl, place the cooked basmati rice as a base.
2. Top with the cooked vegetables, arranging them attractively over the rice.
3. Gently fold in two-thirds of the prepared fruits, reserving the rest for garnish.
4. Drizzle the prepared sauce over everything.
5. Garnish with the remaining fruits, fresh herbs, and toasted sesame seeds.
6. Season with additional salt and pepper to taste.
7. Serve warm or at room temperature.

## Serving Suggestions

This vibrant dish can be served in multiple ways:

1. **Family Style**: Serve in a large, colorful platter in the center of the table for everyone to share.
2. **Individual Bowls**: Create beautiful, individually portioned bowls with rice on the bottom, vegetables and fruits arranged on top, and sauce drizzled over everything.
3. **Side Dish**: Serve smaller portions as a side dish alongside grilled protein of choice.
4. **Cold Salad Version**: Refrigerate leftovers and serve cold the next day as a refreshing salad.

## Storage Instructions

1. **Refrigeration**: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
2. **Freezing**: The vegetable and rice components can be frozen separately for up to 1 month. The fruit component is best not frozen.
3. **Reheating**: Gently reheat in a microwave, covered, for 2-3 minutes, stirring halfway through. Alternatively, reheat in a skillet over medium-low heat with a splash of water or broth to prevent sticking.
